Oyakodon

Description

A delicious Japanese meal consisting of sauteed chicken cooked in a Japanese broth, then finished with egg and served over rice. It's easy to make, and is filling and delicious!

Ingredients

Serves 4

  • 2 cups uncooked jasmine rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken thighs, cut into small pieces
  • 1 onion, halved and sliced
  • 2 cups dashi stock, made with dashi powder
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 tbsp mirin
  • 3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 green onion, chopped (for garnish, optional)

Steps

For the Rice:

  1. Rinse rice in 3 to 4 changes of water until the water is almost clear, then drain off the rinse water.
  2. In a rice cooker, place the rice and 2 cups of water in the pot and cook for about 20 minutes.
  3. Wait for 10 minutes before opening the lid, and fluff the rice with a rice paddle.

For the Chicken and Eggs:

  1. In a nonstick pan with a lid, place the chicken in and stir over medium heat for 5 minutes or until it starts to brown.
  2. Stir in the onion, and cook and stir for another 5 minutes or until the onion is soft.
  3. Pour in the dashi stock, and whisk in soy sauce, mirin and brown sugar, stirring to dissolve the sugar.
  4. Bring the mixture to a boil, and let it simmer for about 10 minutes or until slightly reduced.
  5. Whisk the eggs in a bowl until well-beaten, and pour over the chicken and stock.
  6. Cover the pan, reduce the heat and allow to steam for about 5 minutes or until the egg is cooked. Remove from the heat.

To Serve:

  1. Place 1 cup of cooked rice per bowl into 4 deep soup bowls.
  2. Top each bowl with 1/4 of the chicken and egg mixture, and spoon about 1/2 cup of soup into each bowl.
  3. Optionally, garnish with sliced green onions.
