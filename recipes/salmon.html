Baked Salmon with Lemony Risoni and Basil-Bacon Peas

Description

Risoni, lemon, basil, bacon, peas and baked sockeye salmon all come together in one dish for a quick and easy weeknight dish for the family!

Ingredients

Serves 4

  • 1 cup risoni
  • 2 tsp garlic powder
  • 5 large basil leaves, thinly sliced and divided
  • 2 tsp sea salt, divided
  • 1.5 oz skin-on, sockeye salmon
  • 1/4 cup butter, melted, divided
  • 3 slices bacon, chopped
  • 16 oz frozen peas
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 lemons, juiced, divided

Steps

For the Salmon

  1. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees Fahrenheit (230 degrees Celsius). Line a baking tray with aluminum foil or baking paper.
  2. In a small bowl, mix garlic powder, 4 leaves of basil and 1/2 tsp of salt together.
  3. Place the salmon skin side-down on the pan. Brush with 1 tbsp butter, and sprinkle the garlic-basil mixture on top.
  4. Bake in the oven until the salmon flakes easily with a fork. Remove and let it rest.

For the Peas

  1. Place the bacon in a pan over medium-low heat and cook, stirring regularly for 4 minutes or until crispy.
  2. Add 1/2 tsp salt and peas. Season with pepper.
  3. Cook and stir for 3 to 4 minutes or until the peas are heated through.

For the Risoni

  1. Bring a pot of lightly salted water to a boil.
  2. Cook the risoni in the water, stirring occasionally for about 10 minutes or until al dente.
  3. Drain immediately and set aside.
  4. Whisk the remaining 3 tbsp butter, juice from 1 lemon and the remaining 1 tsp of salt together in a bowl.
  5. Pour over the cooked risoni and toss to coat. Season with pepper.

To Serve

  1. Divide the risoni among 4 plates.
  2. Top with salmon and place peas on the side.
  3. Garnish with the remaining basil.
  4. Drizzle the remaining lemon juice on top.
